Every button changes the NUMBER of data points, not just their values. Entering columns grow from the baseline of their final slot while the survivors slide over on one clock; a removed column shrinks out as a fading ghost; inserting in the middle keeps each datum's identity (bars are matched by category, so existing columns slide instead of morphing into their neighbor's value). On the area chart the fill stays intact mid-transition, markers ride the moving line, and the axis labels travel with their columns. All of this is default behavior of updateOptions() / updateSeries(): no extra config. Rotate values is the same-length control case, which keeps the classic value morph.
