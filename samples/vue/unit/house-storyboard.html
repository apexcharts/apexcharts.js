545 members, one chart

A scroll-driven story on a single unit chart. The same dots start as three party clusters, gather into one chamber, then flow apart into six seniority bars. Scroll back up to rewind. Not one dot is destroyed or created; they only rearrange.

Three parties

The 545 members split into three clusters: 276 Republicans, 266 Democrats and 3 Independents. One dot is one seat.

One chamber

The three clusters gather into a single packed crowd. The tiny Independent group nests in the centre, the parties wrap around it.

By seniority

The same members flow apart into six bars by terms served, each dot travelling from its old spot and recolouring on the way. The freshman bar towers over the veterans.

chart.storyboard.bind({ beats, scroller }) pairs prose elements with views; here scroller points the observer at the story column, so a beat applies as its step crosses the middle of that panel (IntersectionObserver, no scroll listeners) and re-applies in reverse on the way up. Every beat's options payload sets plotOptions.unit.transition: 'flow', which keys the dots by global order: the SAME dot migrates (and recolours) from a party cluster to the packed blob to a seniority bar rather than fading out and back in. Transitions are cut instead of animated under prefers-reduced-motion. The unit chart is a premium type; without a license it renders with a trial watermark. Needs the storyboard feature (bundles perspectives).
